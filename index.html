Machine Learning × Cognitive Science

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and cognitive science. I explore how cognitive principles like bias, reasoning, and belief updating can illuminate how AI systems learn and behave, and how machine learning models can, in turn, help us understand the human mind. My goal is to build interpretable, human-centered AI tools that enhance how people interact with intelligent systems.

Research Themes

  • Human-Centered AI: Designing AI systems grounded in cognitive theory to improve transparency and user experience.
  • Mechanistic Interpretability: Understanding how LLMs represent and update internal beliefs and biases.
  • Cognitive Modeling: Using ML to explain cognitive processes such as anchoring, decision-making, and memory.
  • Responsible AI: Integrating ethics, fairness, and educational outreach in AI development.

Research Projects

Anchoring Bias Mechanisms in GPT-style Models

Investigating how anchoring bias manifests in transformer models using belief-shift metrics (zAA, R-Error, A-Index) and causal tracing. Developing methods to mitigate bias without catastrophic forgetting.

Adversarial Analysis of Membership Inference in LLMs

Building adversarial evaluation pipelines to detect memorization and privacy leaks in LLMs, benchmarking differential privacy and dropout mechanisms.

Bias in English↔Twi Neural Machine Translation

Creating challenge datasets to measure gender bias in translation systems and exploring data augmentation strategies for improved fairness.
